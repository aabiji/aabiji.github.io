# Downfall

I recently just watched [Downfall](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KOFu-sEvwuI&t=3955s). 
The movie's older than me, but it's still great. The thing about this movie is that they
make you have pity for these horrific people who did horrific things.

I know it's just a movie and nothing potrayed is actually real, but the way the movie
describes all the suicides in suck a detailed way is off putting.

The person who stands out the most to me is Fraudl. She witnesses a frail Hitler say the
most horrific things (anti-semitism, etc), she sees the crime scene after he commited suicide, she
knows (maybe not to the full extend) how fucked up everything is, and yet she
doesn't feel like she can do anything about it.

The way Goebbels wife kills all 6 of her children (even when one of her
children could sense something was amiss, and didn't want to take the sleep "medicin")
and clearly shows remorse but carries on coldly anyways, 
then agrees to have her husband (who she clearly no longer has any connection
with), shoot her makes me feel numb. Her devotion to Hitler portrayed in a
vulnerable way is both disturbing and pitying.

The way Eva Brau chooses to dance even through shelling and gunfire, shows
both how naive she so desperately want to be. She knows that facing the truth would be too painful.
Given the movie I'm not sure whether she acutally supported Hitler, or was a
bystander who felt like she could do nothing. How could anybody love a man so
vile and psychopathic, capable of the most evil things.

The way Hitler is portrayed as a sickly old man (with a perpetually trembling
right hand) humanizes him the same way you'd humanize a demon. He has yelling
fits, he's dellusional in victory before realizing it's all over. He thinks he
has the loyalty of his generals, but behind his back they conspire against him
(even with a confusing unwavering loyalty to their oath). He has no
pity for the german people, no regret for dragging them into his (they
supported him after all, in his view, it's a law of nature that the weak
(german civilians) must die). He is clearly both troubled and distant,
offering no depth of personality -- Eva said that even through the 15 years
she's known him, she still knows next to nothing about him. He knows he lied
to the german people, fed them propaganda and yet he feels no remorse.

The little boy fought in the german army, heck Hitler personally gave him a medal.
He witnesses the brutality of war, one minute you're fighting with your
comrads, the next they're dead. He helps one man (temporarily) escape hanging by the SS.
Only to come back home the next night and see his mom shot on the ground and
his dad hanging by the roof. His innocence is gone, he has no where to go, and
in his eyes you can peer into his traumatized soul.

Throughout the movie I was really disturbed, frowning in horror a significant
portion of the time. This movie is horrific, and accurately portayes the
brutality of war and the cold indifference of Hitler and his inner circle.
It's a sombre invitation to never let the atrocities of the past repeat themselves.
