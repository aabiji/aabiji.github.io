# Run you brain single threaded

Multitasking, at least for me, doesn't work at all.
The amount of context switching from one task to another is 
both mentally taxing and unproductive. 

For example, if I make a change to a large codebase and wait
the long compile time (let's say 1 minute), there's a good chance that
I open up hackernews. When I open hackernews. I find an interesting article or blog post,
and spend the next 10 minutes reading it. After that I've completely forgotten
what I'm working on and my train of thought, so I spend the next 5 minutes
trying to remember. Just like that, an edit-compile cycle that should have taken
1 minute took 10 minutes.

If I'm stuck on a particular problem, or I'm trying to fix a nasty bug,
there's a good chance I open Youtube to "think about the problem subconsciously".
I spend the next 30 minutes going through a rabbit hole of entertaining videos. 
This causes me to a) make no progress on the task at hand, and b) raise my dopamine
levels so high that going back to programming seems less appealing.

As a consequence of context switching, I often produce lower quality work. I
take longer to complete a task, and waste my precious time.

So, in the face of long compile times, a non trivial task or a bug, don't do
something else. If that's what it takes, stare at the wall. Anything to truly *focus*.
