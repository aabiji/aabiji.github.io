# Go all in

I have a tendency to tackle things I have a passion for with a fervent dedication.
Whether or not that thing in question will benefit me in any way, and whether
or not it's a waste.

For example, my parents bought this puzzle set for one of my brother's birthdays.
He didn't really use it, so one day when I was particularly bored I took a look at it
and thought *"Well, what if I assembled it?"*. So, for the next month or so,
after school I would assemble this puzzle piece by piece. I would listen to
podcast while assembling the puzzle, I would split my time so that I could work
on other projects, but at the end of the day I made sure that the puzzle was
completed. Once the puzzle was completed, I just moved on to something else.
Sure, I was somewhat happy that the puzzle was asembled beautifully, but I'd
been lured by something else even more exciting.

What's worth doing is worth doing well. If you're willingly taking your time to
do something, but you don't feel passionate enough to go all in with it, then
why do it at all?

The most successful people are successful partly because they went all in with
their ambitions. Every single successful person in really any field understood
what they wanted to do and why, then worked relentlessly towards it.
