# 11 thoughts from 2023

As the year wraps up, I thought I'd share some of the ideas that have really reasonated with me
throughout the year.

1. **What's worth doing is worth doing well.**  
   If you're passionate about something and you want to succeed with it, go all in. 
   A major reason why successful people are successful is because they've went all in with what they are truly passionate about.
   For them, [that's their why](https://www.amazon.ca/Find-Your-Why-Practical-Discovering/dp/0143111728).

2. **Context switching, is the root of all my unproductivity.**  
   Constantly getting bombarded with information is exhausting. It's take a lot
   of self control and getting over a fear of missing out to ignore the constant stream of
   information and just *focus*.

3. **Work is one of the only things that give me true meaning and joy.**  
   Some people live for the weekend and dread work, but I don't. *I love it.*
   Work doesn't and shouldn't define my entire being, but it's still very importatnt
   to me.

4. **Innovation and entertainment are two sides of the same coin.**  
   The desire to innovate and entertain are two equally importatnt aspects of the
   human condition. As much as we need engineers and scientists, we also need artists and writers.
   We need people to drive humanity forwards, not just technologically, but also culturally.

5. **Value infrastructure over product.**  
   A lot of people think that building infrastructure is mundane and boring and that building user
   facing product is where it's at, but I think it's quite the opposite.
   To me, building infrastructure is way more technically challenging. From a
   business perspective is makes sense too. Through the success of other
   businesses that depend on you, you're essentially given enduring relevance in the market.
   Everyone talks about the app, but no one talks
   about all the libraries/frameworks and levels of abstraction that the app
   depends on. I think we should talk more about those dependencies and the people who build them.

6. **We'll get to Mars within my lifetime.**  
   I desperately want us to, anyways.
   Proxima Centauri B is 4.24 light-years (~24.9 trillion miles) away from
   Earth but Mars is only ~140 million miles away.
   That's still a huge distance, but compared to Proxima B, it's trivial. Getting
   to Mars would just be baby steps towards a grander future. I would be so
   honored if I could just play the tiniest part in making that future a reality.

7. **There's an infinite amount of things I don't know that I don't even know.**  
   Everyone, however high the IQ is ignorant in almost everything; after all, ignorance is bliss.
   Stupidity isn't when someone doesn't know something, stupidity is when
   someone doesn't know something and is unwilling to learn.

8. **The world is quite literally at your fingertips.**  
   Information is a google search or llm prompt away. (Also, casually being able to just contact anyone on this
   planet is still something I haven't quite gotten over yet.)
   It sounds really obvious, and as such, I find it frustrating that people around me still expect schools to spoon feed
   them information.

9. **[Nothing is ever done](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bJQj1uKtnus).**  
   No piece of software or wrting is ever "finished".
   There's always more things to add, fix and change. Nothing
   is ever done, it's simply hit a deadline or been abandonned.

10. **Everything's insane and absurd.**  
   Think about it. We're on a wet, floating rock hurling
   through space. We don't know why were here, barely know how we even got here
   and yet were here.

11. **The whole world comes together [to make a single pencil](https://fermatslibrary.com/s/i-pencil).**  
    We take so many things for granted. The shear amount of science, engineering, politics, management, etc needed to
    even be reading this right now is mindbending. Maybe it's cliche, but we truly do
    stand on the shoulder of giants.
