This post describes me going from 40 WPM to 100 WPM.

I used to be really bad at typing. In grade eight the teacher encouraged
us to practice touch typing. I did that for a while, but soon stopped when I
say that everyone had too. Since then, my typing had degenerated into an
inconsistent and inneficient monstrosity.

Some say that typing isn't a bottleneck, but for me it was becomming one.
It disuaded me from writing for extended periods of time because I would think faster
than I think and thus would forget what I wanted to write. It was also
frustrating because every other word would be a typo.

So, I decided that I would reteach myself how to type. I found this [great
website](https://www.keybr.com/) that I've been practicing with. The hardest
part for me was staying consistent with my fingering, unlearning my old habits
and developing the muscle memory for new ones.

Typing fast isn't just a flex. It allows you to interface with software faster,
improving your user expereince and more generally allows you to move faster.
